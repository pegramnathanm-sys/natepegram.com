Ecommerce's Future: Human + AI Agent, Not Headless

Why the future of commerce needs more than APIs, MCPs, and autonomous agents

In recent discussions, headless commerce and AI agents are often touted as the future of ecommerce...

Headless commerce separates the front-end (what you see) from the back-end (product catalogs, checkout, payments) and connects them via APIs. This separation can provide undeniable benefits: faster deployment, omnichannel flexibility, and easy integration across devices and platforms. Brands can deliver highly tailored experiences across websites, mobile apps, wearables, kiosks, and voice assistants.

Meanwhile, agentic commerce (AI agents that can search for products, compare options, fill carts, and complete purchases autonomously) is gaining traction. Major players like Amazon, OpenAI, Perplexity, Walmart, Shopify, Visa, and PayPal, are deploying tools where customers increasingly interact through chatbots instead of visiting storefronts.

But Here's My Take… Retail Therapy Matters, and It Needs More Than APIs or MCP

"Retail therapy" isn't just a playful phrase, it's rooted in real psychology.

Shopping can alleviate stress, boredom, or sadness by restoring personal control, sparking anticipation, and triggering dopamine release even before a purchase.

A NerdWallet article from April 2025 shared a survey that showed 30% of Americans asked spent money and purchased goods to increase their mood. While I can't find the true $ figure associated with retail therapy purchases, I can surely say anecdotally I have been guilty once or ten times buying something to specifically reward me after a long, satisfying week or work.

The joy of browsing, sliding through options, comparing side-by-side, discovering something unexpected, has a therapeutic quality.

It’s not always about the item bought, but the experience... The delight of seeing what's out there, evaluating choices, imagining new possibilities.

Headless and agent-only commerce risk stripping away this human nuance.

The experienced, visual, side-by-side exploration, whether in a digital storefront or a physical mall, is more than functional shopping; it’s an emotional and sensory journey.

And I want to add that for more mundane purchases or repeat transactions such as groceries, toiletries, or other everyday products, I can see fast adoption for agentic commerce. Instead of me making sure that I don't run out of paper towels, I can outsource this to my agentic assistant... and gladly will.

Where I See Opportunities for Human+Agent Harmony

  • Enhanced Digital Merchandising: Websites, when thoughtfully designed, offer layers of storytelling, inspiration, and discovery. That “wandering through shelves” feel also exists online when curated well.
  • Hybrid Interfaces: Imagine agents that don’t just buy, but invite you to browse, displaying products, comparing variants, letting you swipe through looks, then ask, “Want to explore something else?”
  • Retail Therapy via UX: Emotional comfort can be built into interfaces: soft visuals, compelling narratives, ambient browsing, elements that mimic in-store browsing. Omnichannel digital strategies already show how online shops can deliver rich, personalized experiences.
  • Parallel Track: Agents can efficiently handle repeat buys or price-sensitive tasks, but let the websites remain as therapeutic, curated showrooms for exploration.
